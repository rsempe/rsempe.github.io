Around Mont Ventoux & Carpentras

What is there to do near the house? Mont Ventoux, hilltop villages & Provençal markets

Our holiday rentals are ideally located in the heart of the Vaucluse, just 5 minutes from the centre of Carpentras.

Within fifteen minutes to an hour's drive, you can reach Mont Ventoux, the most beautiful hilltop villages in Provence, the lavender fields, the Provençal markets and a host of family activities. Here are our favourites to help you plan your stay.

Mont Ventoux, the Giant of Provence

Visible from the terrace, Mont Ventoux (1 910 m) towers over the whole region. A must-see, whether on foot or by bike.

By bike 🚴

The legendary climb starts from Bédoin (≈ 25 min). A storied Tour de France challenge: we regularly welcome cyclists and provide a space to store bikes safely.

On foot & by car

Hiking trails, a 360° panorama over the Alps and Provence from the summit, and the Nesque gorges on the road to Sault.

Gordes & the hilltop villages

About 35 minutes away, Gordes — listed among the most beautiful villages in France — clings to the cliffside facing the Luberon.

All around: the Sénanque abbey and its lavender, Ménerbes, Lacoste, Bonnieux… a Provençal postcard at every turn.

Roussillon & the Ochre Trail

About 40 minutes away, Roussillon reveals its red and gold cliffs. The Ochre Trail is a short and spectacular walk, perfect for the whole family.

Also worth seeing: the Provençal Colorado of Rustrel, a mineral landscape with canyon-like scenery.

The lavender fields

From mid-June to mid-July, lavender sets the region ablaze. The most beautiful spots: the Sénanque abbey and the Sault plateau.

Distilleries and markets let you take home essential oils and lavender honey.

L'Isle-sur-la-Sorgue & the cool of the water

25 minutes away, the "Venice of Provence" and its canals: famous for its antique dealers and its Sunday market.

Close by, Fontaine-de-Vaucluse (the most powerful spring in France) and the Sorgue river, ideal for a canoe trip in summer.

Markets & gastronomy

The Carpentras market, every Friday morning, is one of the largest in Provence. In winter, Carpentras becomes the capital of the black truffle.

Also worth tasting: the AOC Ventoux wines, the Carpentras berlingot and the Cavaillon melon. The vineyards of the Dentelles de Montmirail (Gigondas, Vacqueyras) are about thirty minutes away.

With the family

Just about ten minutes away: the Wave Island water park and Parc Spirou (rides and a comic-book world).

And on your way back, the villa's private pool, the pétanque court, the table football and the ping-pong table await young and old alike.

Frequently asked questions

Can you cycle up Mont Ventoux from the house?

Yes. The classic climb starts in Bédoin, about 25 minutes away by car. Many cyclists stay with us to conquer the Giant of Provence and store their bikes safely.

What is there to do around Carpentras when it rains?

The covered antique markets of L'Isle-sur-la-Sorgue, the cellars of the AOC Ventoux vineyards, the museums of Avignon or the Wave Island park are all excellent options on a rainy day.

When can you see the lavender fields in bloom?

Lavender usually blooms from mid-June to mid-July, especially near the Sénanque abbey and on the Sault plateau.

What activities are there for children?

The Wave Island water park and Parc Spirou are about 10 minutes away, and the villa has a private pool, a pétanque court, a table football and a ping-pong table.
